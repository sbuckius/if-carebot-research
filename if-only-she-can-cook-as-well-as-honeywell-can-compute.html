✦ The Advertisement Reveals
The Work Was in the Image.
The Performance Made It Visible.
◉ What the Image Conceals
The photograph was designed to make labor disappear. Every adjustment of posture, breath, and expression was performed precisely so that no trace of effort would remain in the final frame. The image is the product of that erasure — a record of what was hidden, not what was done.
◈ What the Performance Revealed
The live reenactment reintroduced time, breath, and effort into what the photograph had flattened into a single frozen moment. Watching a body hold that pose — hearing the strain named aloud — made visible what advertising is designed to conceal: that the effortlessness is the hardest work of all.
◆ When AI Narrates Human Labor
The AI voice could identify physical data — angle of spine, tension in jaw, duration of hold — but not the experience of performing it. The machine described the labor. The human body bore it. That gap between description and experience is where the question of automation becomes most urgent. Read the document ↗ — the AI's full analysis, presentation labor score, and performance script.
✦ About This Work
This performance was created by an artist who believes technology is not neutral — that every system reflects the people who build it, the assumptions they carry, and the voices they include or exclude. The same computational infrastructure that produced this advertisement in 1969 was built overwhelmingly by people who did not look like the woman in the image — and the AI systems that analyzed and narrated her labor today were built by many of the same kinds of people, with many of the same blind spots.

This work is part of a broader commitment: to make visible the diversity of bodies, experiences, and forms of knowledge that technology has historically erased; to resist the story that progress is inevitable, neutral, and directionless; and to ask instead — progress toward what, and for whom? The alternative this work proposes is not less technology, but technology oriented around care: careful of whose labor it depends on, careful of whose knowledge it draws from, careful of what it makes invisible in the act of making things visible.
