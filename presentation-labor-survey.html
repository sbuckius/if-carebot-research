✦ The Survey Reveals
The Labor Was Always There.
It Was Never Listed.
◉ The Invisible Calculation
The advertisement promises the computer will help her calculate. She is already calculating — continuously, without the computer, without recognition. Posture, expression, breath, weight, gaze: 40 simultaneous systems, all invisible in the final image.
◈ The Cost That Was Not Listed
The computer cost $10,600. The apron cost $28. Her name, her labor, her calibrated smile — the emotion conversion from fatigue to elegance, from anxiety to calm competence — cost was not listed. It was the most essential work in the image.
◆ What Machines Cannot Do
The Honeywell H316 could compute menus. It could not perform the presentation labor that sold it. The work that is hardest to automate — embodied, relational, emotional — remains the work that is least compensated and least recognized.
