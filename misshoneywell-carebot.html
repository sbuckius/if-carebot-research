✦ Critical Context · Read Before Asking
⚠️ Is this a robot — or is it a woman doing unpaid performance labour?
Honeywell presented Miss Honeywell as "the ultimate in automation" and "the human-tronic saleswoman of tomorrow" at the Instruments, Electronics and Automation Exhibition at Olympia. The narrator asks: "Is this computerized control or is it a conjuring trick?" The answer is neither. The firm behind her is Magical Productions — run by a Hollywood conjurer named Mark Wilson — and Honeywell paid £2,000 to bring her over from Hollywood. When challenged to prove she was a robot by removing her head, he closed the cabinet, citing "delicate micro circuits." Whatever Miss Honeywell was, she was scripted to say she'd make "someone a very good wife." A machine defined by domesticity. A woman defined as a machine. The ambiguity was the whole point.
